Signs that you need to repair or replace your water pumps.

The water pumps are designed to ensure a constant flow of water and equal water pressure across your dishwasher, kitchen & bathroom sink, and shower. Over time your water pumps might not work or show some problems.
If you are experiencing one of these symptoms with your water pump then it might be the right time to repair or replace your water pumps.

  • No water coming through kitchen taps or showerhead
  • Humming sound or big noise from the pump.
  • Variation in water pressure across taps
  • Water spitting out of the faucet
  • High electricity bills

Water Pump Replacement

A water pump usually does not require serious attention unless you have bought the right one. Most of the times water pumps work continuously for days without you even noticing it. They are that silent. Due to this, they are often ignored until you notice some noise or faulty symptoms. A good water pump can perform continuously and reliably for a long period.
On average a water pump can last between 8 to 15 years. If your water pump has served you more than the average life span and you are experiencing faults with it, then it's time to replace it with a new one. Even though repairing the water pump is an option, we don’t recommend repairing it if it’s more than 10 years old. An old water pump will always be prone to repairs thus wasting your time and money.
If you want to replace the water pumps we can help you with it. Firstly we will assess the required water pressure and flow rate, the distance between the water tank & your property, and the current plumping system. We will share the assessment details with you and recommend a new efficient water pump to replace your old one.
